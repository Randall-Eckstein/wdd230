Demographics

Jilin is the second-largest city and the former capital of Jilin province in northeast China.

As of the 2020 census, 5,519,578 people live in the city and four urban districts.

History

During the Ming Dynasty (1368 - 1644), mighty river fleets were built and sailed several times from Jilin City. Due to the geographic protections and location, it proved a safe and secure harbor.

As the Russian Empire advanced eastward to the Pacific coast, the Qing government (1636 - 1912) ordered a naval shipbuilding factory set up in Jilin in 1661.

Jilin was officially established as a fort city in 1673.

A poem written in 1682 by Kangxi Emperor while visiting Jilin gave the city the nickname "River City."

After the illigitmate Manchukuo regime established their capital in present-day Changchun (the largest city of Jilin province) in 1932, Jilin's importance decreased.

Jilin became the provincial capital of Jilin Province after the establishment of the People's Republic of China in 1949, until Changchun took this position in 1956.

Climate

Jilin has a four-season, monsoon influenced climate.

Winters are long, lasting from November to March, cold and windy, but dry.

Tourism

Jilin's main winter attraction is the rime ice on trees along the banks of the Songhua River.

Main Attractions in Jilin City:

  • Meteorite Museum
  • Beishan Park (North Hill park)
  • Dragon Pool Mountain Park
  • Songhua Lake (south-east of Jilin)
  • Wulajie Old City. A Qing Dynasty walled town on the east bank of the Songhua River.
  • BeiDaHu Ski Village (south of Jilin)

Transportation

Air

The city is served by Changchun Longjia International Airport located about 76 km (47 miles) away from Jilin City.

Railway

Jilin is served by the Jilin Railway Station which provides convenient access to many cities around China including Beijing, Tianjin, Dalian, Jinan, Hangzhou, Harbin, Changchun, and Shenyang.
